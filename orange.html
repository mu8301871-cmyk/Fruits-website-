Orange!

An orange is a round, sweet, and juicy citrus fruit with a bright orange peel that grows on trees in warm climates around the world. Oranges are a major source of vitamin C and are rich in fiber, potassium, and other nutrients that benefit health. People eat oranges raw, and they are also used to make juice, marmalades, sauces, and orange oil. Nutritional content Oranges are an excellent source of vitamin C, which is important for immune function and overall health. They also provide fiber, which helps regulate blood sugar and cholesterol levels. Other vitamins and minerals found in oranges include thiamin, folate, potassium, and calcium. Varieties and growth There are over 600 varieties of oranges, with the sweet orange being the most common. The fruit originated in southeastern Asia, but is now grown in many warm regions globally. Orange trees are typically about 20 feet tall and have glossy green leaves and white flowers. Uses and consumption Oranges are eaten fresh, with the juicy pulp being consumed after peeling the rind. They are widely used to make orange juice, which can be purchased fresh or frozen. The peel is also used for its fragrance in products like essential oils, and for flavoring in foods like marmalade.
